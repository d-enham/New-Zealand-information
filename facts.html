Top 3 Facts About New Zealand

New Zealand has a rich and tragic history; here are some facts about it!

1. Last major landmass settled by humans

New Zealand was the last major habitable landmass to be settled by humans, with Polynesian ancestors of the Māori arriving via voyaging canoes around 1250–1300 CE. Located in the Southwest Pacific, this remote archipelago remained untouched until this late migration, with subsequent significant European settlement beginning in the 1840s

2. Worlds Clearest Water

Rotomairewhenua (Blue Lake) in New Zealand's Nelson Lakes National Park is recognized as the clearest natural freshwater body in the world, with horizontal visibility of up to 80 meters, nearly as clear as distilled water.

3. More Sheep than People?

New Zealand maintains one of the highest sheep-to-human ratios in the world, with approximately 4.6 to 5 sheep per person as of 2023–2024, down from a peak of 22 per person in 1982.
